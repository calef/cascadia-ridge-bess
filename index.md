Jupiter Power LLC is proposing a 130 MW lithium-ion battery energy storage facility on roughly 45 acres in unincorporated King County, directly across Snoqualmie Parkway from Snoqualmie Ridge. The site would connect to PSE's Mt. Si substation via a 115kV tie-line.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
**We support grid storage.** PSE needs it for CETA compliance — Washington requires utilities to be greenhouse gas neutral by 2030 and 100% clean by 2045. Battery storage is part of how we get there. We understand why this site was chosen: the Mt. Si substation is right next door, and undeveloped land next to existing transmission infrastructure is rare. Grid storage here strengthens reliability for the Eastside, including Snoqualmie Ridge. We want that benefit.

**The issue is what kind of battery, and what protections come with it.** Lithium-ion at this site creates fire, noise, visual, and environmental risks that are solvable with technology Jupiter Power has already invested in.
</div>

## What we're asking for

1. **Sodium-ion or equivalent non-lithium-ion chemistry.** Jupiter Power signed a $500 million sodium-ion deal with Peak Energy in 2025. Their CEO said it "checks every box." Sodium-ion eliminates thermal runaway, eliminates toxic hydrogen fluoride emissions, and eliminates 24/7 cooling fan noise. The facility won't operate until late 2028 — sodium-ion will be commercially available well before then.

2. **Tree retention on the west side of the site.** Critical area buffers protect the north and east edges, but the Parkway-facing side — the side the community sees — has no protection. Keeping the existing trees costs the project nothing.

## Why this matters

Using the industry's own data (EPRI), a lithium-ion BESS has roughly a **1-in-23 chance of a fire with safety concerns over 15 years** of operation. That's at the optimistic 0.3% annual rate. At a more realistic 0.5%, it's about 1-in-14. [Read the full analysis.](/fire-risk)

This facility would sit across the parkway from thousands of homes, next to Fisher Creek (salmon habitat), in a valley where temperature inversions trap smoke. Sodium-ion takes thermal runaway off the table entirely.

## Current status

No active permit application as of March 2026. Jupiter Power's previous application (filed December 2025) was canceled in February 2026. Jupiter Power held a community open house on March 17, 2026.
