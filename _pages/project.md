## What the facility would include

- Lithium-ion batteries in storage containers
- Thermal management / cooling systems (fans, HVAC — operate 24/7)
- Fire detection and suppression equipment
- Inverters, transformers, and substation
- Perimeter fencing and security gates
- 115kV generator tie-line to Mt. Si substation

## Jupiter Power

Founded 2017. Acquired by BlackRock's Diversified Infrastructure business in late 2022. About 8,000 MWh operating or under construction with an 11,000+ MW pipeline.

Also developing Blackberry Grove (100 MW) near Hillsboro, Oregon, which has drawn organized opposition from the No Batteries In Backyards group.

In 2025, Jupiter Power signed a **$500 million / 4.75 GWh supply agreement with Peak Energy for sodium-ion batteries.** CEO Andy Bowman said the technology "checks every box" for safety and cost. This is one of the largest sodium-ion procurement deals globally — and it's the same company proposing lithium-ion for Snoqualmie Ridge.

## PSE tolling agreement

PSE filed a voluntary All-Source RFP in July 2024 (UTC Docket UE-240532) for CETA-compliant resources. The RFP received 98 proposals. The Cascadia Ridge tolling agreement was executed August 22, 2025 and filed with the UTC on September 19, 2025. Financial terms are confidential in their entirety.

PSE needs storage to meet CETA requirements: up to 2.3 million annual MWh from renewable/nonemitting resources by 2030, and up to 1,755 MW summer peak capacity by 2029. Without storage, PSE must either overbuild generation (expensive, passed to ratepayers) or maintain fossil fuel peakers (violates CETA).

## Community opposition

Residents spoke against the project at the March 9, 2026 Snoqualmie City Council meeting, citing proximity to neighborhoods and parks, fire risk, noise, air and light pollution, and impacts on fish-bearing streams.

Councilmember Dan Murphy: "Industrial battery storage doesn't belong in the middle of a neighborhood."

Snoqualmie's Comprehensive Plan designates this site for "master-planned business park" and "innovative mixed use" — not industrial battery storage. The Comp Plan identifies utility/power generation near the city's sewer treatment plant off Millpond Road near SR 202.
